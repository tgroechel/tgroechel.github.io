PoseToCode is a web application where students create code blocks by doing different poses. The code blocks allow the robot to do actions.

What is augmented reality?

Augmented reality is the ability to project and interact with 3D virtual objects in the physical world. You may be able to try out a form of augmented reality on your phone right now! If you download the Google App on your iPhone or use Google on Android, you can view animals in the space around you. See the video below for an example of how to do so. What is Kuri? And what is a robot tutor?

Kuri, created by Mayfield Robotics in 2017, is a mobile robot designed as a companion robot for your home. While the company sadly closed, they gave many of their Kuri's away for research purposes. Kuri is about 2 feet tall and expresses most of its emotions through its eyes. We have created a 3D augmented reality model of Kuri that acts similar to the physical robot. Within MoveToCode, Kuri acts as a tutor and guide during the interaction. The field of robot tutors is growing with companies starting to deploy them within schools to help assist teachers for personalized student interactions.
